MY STORY

Ten years ago, my husband, two daughters and I spent Thanksgiving with friends down in Washington D.C. Just before I fell asleep that night, I prayed for insight into the complex and interpersonal struggles my husband and I were facing. Therapy, arguments, and miscommunication left us both steeped in frustration. In the early hours of the following morning, everyone in the house was still fast asleep. I awoke from a vivid dream where my husband was no longer who I thought he was. And I had irrevocably changed as well. It was time. An unmistakable feeling of fear ran from my feet all the way up through my body and out my head.

Later that day, I asked for a separation. We endured a six-hour drive home in silence and pain. The weeks that followed were heart-wrenching as we privately discussed custody of our children. On the outside, I felt utterly unprepared. We were both terrified. I made a choice. To walk through this with dignity and grace, I must figure out a way to mainline my intuition.

I started to wake at 5AM each morning. Thought a series of guided meditations, I breathed life into my inner child, who I named MLG which stood for “My Little Girl.” I gave her a desperately needed voice. She would soon take me and all sorts of adventures, showing me her light side and more importantly, the dark so that I could accept her and help her to heal. Many meditations ended with a new realization about who I had been and who I was becoming. More importantly, they addressed a deep fear and sadness that I had been carrying and would need to face in the coming months en route to transitioning to a single parent.

Following each meditation, I journaled, then drew what they -felt- like. It occurred to me that MLG was telling me a story, so I created paintings to illustrate her adventures about leaving her familiar island for a new city, on a new island. I called the series, "New City" and started to show it at private and local events.

To say that meditation, visual journaling and painting kept me sane is an understatement. They helped me tap into a sub-conscious force and internal guidance that I couldn't otherwise access. We are guiding ourselves, together and independently, we are each entirely unique, and it is an ongoing process that we get to put into practice. The creative process initiates internal abundance.

I practice this today. In fact, I recently journaled, sketched and painted my way into launching my daughter off to college. The creative process allows us to tap into what we already know about ourselves. We have an incredible intuition and sacred knowledge available that leads us through obscure and complicated life work. We access our own intuitive understanding through art. Today I will share some of the tools I use in my own life and with my clients to reconnect ourselves to what we already know.
